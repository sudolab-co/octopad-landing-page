A future where humans and AI work together, naturally.

The next decade of work is not about replacing humans with agents. It's about humans and AI sharing the same workspace, the same context, and the same source of truth, so each does what it does best.

The honest truth is that AI today is not strong enough to do impactful work on its own. Teams betting on full autonomy keep getting burned. What actually works is symbiosis.

You set direction and make the calls. Your AI handles the structured work: pulling context, drafting plans, capturing decisions, keeping state current. Both of you contribute to the same shared brain.

That is the future we're building toward. Agent delegation, when it matures, will extend this foundation, not replace the humans at the center of it.

Where we're going.

Right now, our focus is nailing the fundamentals of the AI back office. Context that flows, plans that hold across sessions, a shared brain that stays current as work happens. Get those three right, and everything else gets easier.

Next, we're integrating autonomous agents into the middle of the system, so the back office gets smarter and more proactive on its own. Noticing slipping deadlines, flagging stale decisions, keeping plans current without a human pushing every button.

We're also experimenting with agent delegation. The idea is simple: you brainstorm a full plan with an AI (code or business, either works), then hand the individual steps off to specialized agents. Each one picks up with the back office context already loaded, runs its part at the highest degree of efficiency, and reports back into the same shared brain. You keep the strategy and the final call. Agents run the structured work underneath. You gain real time back, and you ship with a high degree of confidence in what lands.
