Getting Started with Octopad

This guide walks you through how Octopad works, from setup to your daily rhythm. No prior knowledge assumed.

Shortcut worth knowing: once your AI is connected, you can ask it anything you read here. "What is a work stream?" "How do I capture a decision?" Your AI already knows. This guide is the printed backup. Your AI is the live tutor.

1. What Octopad is in 60 seconds

Octopad is your AI's back-office: your strategy, your tasks, your plans, your decisions, and your team's know-how, all in one shared brain your AI can read, write, and work from.

You work mostly by talking to your AI. The AI creates tasks, wires dependencies, captures decisions, writes pages, ties work to strategy. The Octopad interface is a control panel where you see what your AI does and steer when needed. You can click around and build things by hand, but the AI is the faster path and produces richer output for the same effort.

Why this matters. A normal AI chat has no back-office. Every new conversation starts cold. Octopad gives every session a shared brain with everything your team has captured, and a structured place to capture what comes next. Nothing gets re-explained. Nothing gets lost.

2. Set up

Seven small moves. About five minutes.

Sign up.

Free, no credit card. Head to octopad.app.

Follow the onboarding.

It walks you through connecting your AI and setting up your workspace. Come back here when it's done.

Open your first chat and say "use octopad."

Start a new conversation with your AI and type it exactly: "use octopad." Your AI connects to your workspace and is ready to work inside Octopad. Your workspace is still mostly empty; you're about to fill it.

Give your AI information about your company and your product.

In that same chat, share the basics. Your AI turns what you share into pages inside Octopad, typically a Product Overview and a Company Overview. These become your first reference documents.

Turn on "add to workspace context" for those pages.

As a workspace admin, flip the toggle on your Product Overview and Company Overview. Every teammate's AI will now load those summaries at the start of every session, so no one on the team has to re-explain the basics. (There's a per-user version of the toggle too, for pages you only want in your own context.)

Verify it loads.

Close that chat and open a fresh one. Say "use octopad" and ask a question about what you just shared: "what does our product do?" or "who are we building for?" Your AI should read it back.

Set your first strategy.

Ask your AI to create at least one goal and one work stream. For example: a goal like "Launch our beta by June 30" and a work stream under it called "Marketing Website." Goals and streams are how your AI understands where any task fits. A task on its own is just a line item. A task inside a stream, under a goal, has purpose.

3. How your AI finds the right context

Your AI's context in Octopad sits in three systems, working together. Your AI handles them for you. Knowing what's under the hood helps you get more out of the product.

Pages.

Long-form documents. This is where your core reference content lives: Product Overview, Company Overview, Brand Guidelines, strategy notes, research, meeting notes, anything your AI should be able to pull from later. Every page gets an auto-generated summary, so your AI can skim the index before opening any page in full.

Key Information.

Short, atomic items. Four kinds: a fact ("we ship on Fridays"), a decision ("we chose Stripe over Paddle for native payouts"), an open question ("do we need SOC 2 before Q4?"), and a risk ("our top competitor just raised"). Your AI captures these automatically as they come up in conversation, and pulls the relevant ones back when it picks up a task later.

Build Context.

The engine that assembles the right material for each job. When you ask your AI to work on a task, it calls build context. That pulls the task description, its dependencies, its linked pages, related key information, the tracker page for its work stream (more on trackers in the next section), and additional page chunks that match the task semantically, even if they were never linked directly. Your AI shows up already briefed.

An example. You ask your AI to start working on the "Create the pricing page" task. Build context loads the task, your Brand Guidelines page, last week's Value Propositions page, the decision about positioning you captured on Monday, and a few relevant chunks from other pages in your workspace. Your AI starts writing already knowing everything you know.

4. How work is organized

Four concepts, stacked from biggest to smallest.

Organizations and Workspaces.

An organization is your company. Workspaces live inside it. You invite people at the org level, then grant them access to specific workspaces.

We suggest starting with one workspace. Context isn't shared across workspaces yet, so one workspace keeps everything in one brain. You can always split later.

Roles are simple. At the org level: Owner (full control, including deleting the org), Admin (invites members, creates workspaces, manages settings), Member (access only to workspaces they're explicitly invited to). Inside each workspace: Admin (manages members and integrations) and Contributor (creates and edits everything: tasks, pages, knowledge).

Goals.

What you're trying to achieve and why. Quarterly or yearly. For example: "Launch our beta by June 30."

Work Streams.

How you get there. A stream is a project or an area of work. Two flavors:

  • Ongoing streams never close. They cover permanent areas like "Customer Support" or "Content."
  • Time-bound streams have a target date and a finish line. They sit under a goal, for example "Marketing Website" under "Launch our beta by June 30."

Every stream gets an auto-generated tracker page that logs activity and progress summaries on its own. You don't update it by hand.

Tasks.

The unit of work. Each task has a Why (the problem), a What (the scope), and a How (the approach, when it's non-obvious). Your AI also adds a "Done when" line so you know when it's really finished. Tasks have priority, impact, can have subtasks, and can depend on other tasks with a one-line rationale so the order is never mysterious.

A note on task standards.

Your AI follows a template when it creates tasks, which is why they're richer than what you'd type by hand in a hurry. The template isn't user-customizable yet. We're always looking to make Octopad better, so if you want to change what the template includes, tell us.

AI score.

Every task gets a score. It combines priority, impact, deadline pressure, and where the task sits in the dependency chain. It surfaces what's most important to work on next, so you don't have to scan the whole backlog. Read it as a useful recommendation.

Automatic rituals.

When a task closes, your AI writes a completion comment: what shipped, why that approach, any follow-up. When a time-bound stream finishes, your AI writes a closing report. These compound. A month in, your workspace is a real record of what got done and why.

A full example. You're launching the beta of your product. Goal: "Launch our beta by June 30." Work stream under it: "Marketing Website." Tasks inside: "Write the homepage copy," "Set up email capture," "Record the demo video," "Publish the site." Subtasks break the bigger ones down. Dependencies wire the order ("Write the homepage copy" before "Record the demo video," since the video echoes the copy). Your AI picks what to work on next based on the score. You can always override.

5. The "use octopad" handshake

Every new chat with your AI starts cold unless you tell it otherwise. Say "use octopad" at the start of any fresh conversation and your AI loads a package from your workspace before doing any real work.

What's in the package:

  • Your core pages (the ones you toggled into workspace context).
  • A quick strategy briefing: your active goals and your open work streams.
  • A short recap of your last three sessions, so your AI knows what you were doing recently.
  • The Octopad playbook: how to capture decisions, how to create good tasks, when to update a tracker, how to close things out. This is what keeps your AI consistent across conversations.

When to say it. Every time you start a new chat with your AI, before you ask for any real work. If you forget, your AI will still try, but the quality drops because it's flying without the briefing.

6. Running your day

Your to-do list.

Your personal, ordered queue of what you're working on now. Two ways to fill it. Ask your AI to generate it ("what should I work on today?") and it picks up to five tasks based on priority, dependencies, and deadlines. Or pin specific tasks yourself ("add the homepage copy task to my to-do list").

Resume one thing.

Say "resume working on my to-do list" and your AI runs you through the queue in order. Say "resume working on the homepage copy" to jump straight to one task. If that task has subtasks, your AI automatically picks the right next one. You don't need to specify.

7. Our little helpers in the background

Octopad has a small fleet of always-on AI agents we call octobots. They run in the background and keep your workspace tidy without anyone triggering them. A few examples:

  • Session Scribe writes a short summary of what happened every time a session ends, so the next one picks up cleanly.
  • Page Summarizer keeps every page's summary fresh as you edit, so your AI can skim before diving in.
  • Stream Activity Logger writes one entry per work stream every time something changes, so your tracker pages stay current.
  • Work Stream Reconciler runs nightly and compresses the day's activity into a clean progress report on each active stream.

You don't interact with them. They just do their job. We're actively building more to take even more routine work off your plate and make the system smarter over time.

8. Chain with your other tools

Octopad is one connector in a bigger picture. Your AI can talk to many other apps the same way: Slack, Gmail, GitHub, Tiny Fish, your calendar, and more. When you connect more than one, your AI can work across all of them in a single conversation.

Three examples you can say out loud to your AI today:

  • "Scan our #product channel in Slack for anything that should become a task in Octopad." Your AI reads the last week of messages, picks what's actionable, creates tasks in the right stream, and links back to the original messages.
  • "Create a task for John to review the pricing page, then send him a Slack message explaining why I'm assigning it." Your AI creates the task in Octopad, then sends John a short message in Slack with the context, so he knows the what and why before he even opens Octopad.
  • "Ask Tiny Fish to run a competitor analysis and log the findings to a page in Octopad." Tiny Fish runs the research; your AI takes the results and writes them up as a page in the right stream, with sources attached.

Each of these is one sentence. No copy-paste between tabs, no "where did I save that." Your AI becomes the connective tissue between your tools, and Octopad is where the durable context lives so nothing falls through the cracks.

9. The right mindset, and what's next

AI-first.

Octopad is designed so you talk to your AI, and the interface catches what the AI does. You can absolutely click around and build things by hand, but when you ask your AI to do it, you get richer descriptions, better-linked pages, cleaner dependencies, and captured decisions, all for the same amount of effort on your part. Treat the interface as a control panel: a place to see, steer, and occasionally click.

Your AI is the tutor.

Anything in this guide, your AI can explain on demand. Ask: "what is a work stream?" or "how does build context work?" or "how do I archive a task?" If something feels confusing, the answer is one sentence away.
