1 — What Octopad is in 60 seconds

Octopad is your AI's back-office: your strategy, your tasks, your plans, your decisions, and your team's know-how, all in one shared brain your AI can read, write, and work from.

You work mostly by talking to your AI. The AI creates tasks, wires dependencies, captures decisions, writes pages, ties work to strategy. The Octopad interface is a control panel where you see what your AI does and steer when needed.

Why this matters: a normal AI chat has no back-office. Every new conversation starts cold. Octopad gives every session a shared brain with everything your team has captured, and a structured place to capture what comes next.

2 — Set up

Seven small moves. About five minutes.

Sign up.

Free, no credit card. Head to octopad.app.

Follow the onboarding.

It walks you through connecting your AI and setting up your workspace. Come back here when it's done.

Open your first chat and say "use octopad."

Start a new conversation with your AI and type it exactly: "use octopad." Your AI connects to your workspace and is ready to work inside Octopad.

Give your AI information about your company and your product.

In that same chat, share the basics. Your AI turns what you share into pages inside Octopad, typically a Product Overview and a Company Overview.

Turn on "add to workspace context" for those pages.

As a workspace admin, flip the toggle on your Product Overview and Company Overview. Every teammate's AI will now load those summaries at the start of every session.

Verify it loads.

Close that chat and open a fresh one. Say "use octopad" and ask a question about what you just shared. Your AI should read it back.

Set your first strategy.

Ask your AI to create at least one goal and one work stream. For example: a goal like "Launch our beta by June 30" and a work stream under it called "Marketing Website."

3 — How your AI finds the right context

Your AI's context in Octopad sits in three systems, working together.

Pages.

Long-form documents. Product Overview, Company Overview, Brand Guidelines, strategy notes, research, meeting notes. Every page gets an auto-generated summary, so your AI can skim the index before opening any page in full.

Key Information.

Short, atomic items. Four kinds: a fact ("we ship on Fridays"), a decision ("we chose Stripe over Paddle"), an open question ("do we need SOC 2 before Q4?"), and a risk ("our top competitor just raised"). Your AI captures these automatically as they come up.

Build Context.

The engine that assembles the right material for each job. When you ask your AI to work on a task, it pulls the task description, its dependencies, linked pages, related key information, and additional page chunks that match semantically. Your AI shows up already briefed.

4 — How work is organized

Four concepts, stacked from biggest to smallest.

Organizations and Workspaces.

An organization is your company. Workspaces live inside it. We suggest starting with one workspace — context isn't shared across workspaces yet, so one workspace keeps everything in one brain.

Goals.

What you're trying to achieve and why. Quarterly or yearly.

Work Streams.

How you get there. Ongoing streams (permanent areas like "Customer Support") or time-bound streams (with a target date, sitting under a goal). Every stream gets an auto-generated tracker page.

Tasks.

The unit of work. Each task has a Why (the problem), a What (the scope), and a How (the approach). Your AI also adds a "Done when" line. Tasks have priority, impact, subtasks, and dependencies.

5 — The "use octopad" handshake

Every new chat starts cold unless you tell it otherwise. Say "use octopad" at the start of any fresh conversation and your AI loads a package from your workspace:

  • Your core pages (the ones you toggled into workspace context).
  • A quick strategy briefing: active goals and open work streams.
  • A recap of your last three sessions.
  • The Octopad playbook: how to capture decisions, create tasks, and close things out.

6 — Running your day

Your to-do list.

Your personal, ordered queue. Ask your AI to generate it ("what should I work on today?") or pin specific tasks yourself.

Resume one thing.

Say "resume working on my to-do list" and your AI runs you through the queue in order. Or "resume working on the homepage copy" to jump straight to one task.

7 — Octobots

A small fleet of always-on AI agents that run in the background and keep your workspace tidy:

  • Session Scribe — writes a short summary when each session ends.
  • Page Summarizer — keeps every page's summary fresh as you edit.
  • Stream Activity Logger — writes one entry per stream when something changes.
  • Work Stream Reconciler — runs nightly and compresses the day's activity into a clean progress report.

8 — Chain with your other tools

Octopad is one connector in a bigger picture. Your AI can talk to Slack, Gmail, GitHub, Tiny Fish, your calendar, and more. Three examples you can say to your AI today:

  • "Scan our #product channel in Slack for anything that should become a task in Octopad."
  • "Create a task for John to review the pricing page, then send him a Slack message explaining why."
  • "Ask Tiny Fish to run a competitor analysis and log the findings to a page in Octopad."

9 — The right mindset

AI-first.

Octopad is designed so you talk to your AI, and the interface catches what the AI does. You can click around and build things by hand, but when you ask your AI to do it, you get richer output for the same effort.

Your AI is the tutor.

Anything in this guide, your AI can explain on demand. Ask: "what is a work stream?" or "how does build context work?" The answer is one sentence away.

Tell us what's missing.

If something doesn't make sense or you wish Octopad worked differently, tell us. There's a feedback button in every workspace.
