Quick start · 5 minutes

Octopad, from zero.

This walks you through creating a workspace, connecting one AI, and running your first plan — end to end. You'll be productive today.

1 — Install

Octopad runs in your browser, but pairs with a tiny local agent that your AI tools already know how to talk to.

# macOS & Linux
curl -fsSL https://octopad.ai/install | sh

# Windows (PowerShell)
iwr https://octopad.ai/install.ps1 -useb | iex

The installer drops a binary in ~/.octopad and adds it to your PATH. Verify:

octopad --version
# 0.4.2

2 — Create a workspace

A workspace is your team's shared brain. Think of it as one folder your AIs can all read and write to.

octopad init my-team
cd my-team
octopad open

That opens your workspace in the browser. You'll see an empty shared brain and a prompt for your first task.

3 — Connect an AI

Octopad doesn't run inference itself. You bring your own model keys — we pass them straight to Anthropic, OpenAI, or whoever you use.

Claude

octopad connect claude --key $ANTHROPIC_API_KEY

ChatGPT

octopad connect openai --key $OPENAI_API_KEY

Cursor

Cursor connects via the local agent — no key needed. Just click Settings → Integrations → Cursor.

Your keys live on your machine. Octopad never sees inference payloads.

4 — Your first plan

In the workspace, press ⌘ K and describe an outcome — not a list of steps. Octopad will turn it into a plan you can edit.

  1. Type: "Draft the launch email for v0.4 and schedule it in Gmail."
  2. Review the 4-step plan that appears.
  3. Click Run — or add a checkpoint first (Octopad will pause for your approval).

The task runs in the background. Come back to it from any device — the shared brain remembers everything.

5 — Invite the team

octopad invite alex@team.com emma@team.com

Teammates join with one click. Their sessions pick up where yours left off — same context, same decisions, same open threads.
